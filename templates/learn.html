Opportunity Cost Engine helps you understand the hidden financial trade-offs behind everyday purchases. Before you buy a product, you can see what could happen if you instead invest the same amount of money in the company that makes it, or another company of your choice.

Our goal is to make financial thinking simple, clear, and accessible to everyone. This helps you understand the opportunity cost - the value of the choice you didn't make.

Key Concepts Explained

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost means the benefits you miss out on when choosing one option over another. For example, if you spend £500 on a phone, the opportunity cost is what that £500 could have earned if you had invested it instead.

Investment Ticker

A ticker is simply a short code used to identify a company's stock. For example, AAPL represents Apple.

Prediction Model

A prediction model is a method used to estimate how an investment might perform in the future. We use three beginner-friendly models to help show how your money could grow under different conditions.

Our Three Forecasting Strategies

1. CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)

This strategy looks at how much the stock has grown per year on average. It's simple and helps you see steady long-term growth.

2. Geometric Brownian Motion

This model adds randomness to reflect how prices move up and down over time. It’s useful for showing a more realistic (but still simplified) picture of uncertainty in markets.

3. News Sentiment Model

This method uses the tone of recent news to estimate whether a stock might behave positively or negatively. If news is mostly positive, the model leans toward better performance, and vice versa.
